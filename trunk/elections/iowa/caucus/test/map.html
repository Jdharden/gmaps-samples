On January 3, Democratic and Republican voters will gather in Iowa for precinct caucuses. Unlike the the primary elections with secret ballots in other states, the Iowa caucuses are open meetings with votes taken by paper ballot, a show of hands, or by standing in groups for each candidate.

Iowa is the first state in the nation to vote on candidates, often guiding the primary vote for the rest of the United States.
